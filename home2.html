Introduction to Engineering

Engineering is the application of science and mathematics to solve problems. Engineers figure out how things work and find practical uses for scientific discoveries. Scientists and inventors often get the credit for innovations that advance the human condition, but it is engineers who are instrumental in making those innovations available to the world.

Fields of Engineering

Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical engineering is one of the broadest engineering disciplines. Mechanical engineers design, develop, build, and test mechanical and thermal sensors and devices.

Electrical Engineering

Electrical engineers design, develop, and test electrical equipment, including electric motors, radar and navigation systems, communications systems, and power generation equipment.

Computer Engineering

Computer engineering is a discipline that integrates several fields of computer science and electronics engineering required to develop computer hardware and software.

Famous Engineers

  • Nikola Tesla: Known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.
  • Thomas Edison: Inventor of the phonograph, the motion picture camera, and the electric light bulb.
  • Alan Turing: Mathematician and computer scientist who played a crucial role in the development of theoretical computer science.
  • Elon Musk: Known for his work in electric vehicles, space exploration, and renewable energy.

Fun Facts about Engineering

  • Engineers design and build the world around us, including buildings, roads, bridges, and other infrastructure.
  • The word "engineer" is derived from the Latin word "ingenium," meaning "cleverness."
  • Leonardo da Vinci, known as an artist, was also a prolific engineer and inventor.
  • The first engineer known by name is Imhotep, a chancellor to the Egyptian pharaoh Djoser and the architect of the Pyramid of Djoser.
